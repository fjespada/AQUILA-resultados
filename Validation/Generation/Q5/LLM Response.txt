Meta-Solving is a promising approach to hybridize quantum and classical computing. It involves using a meta-solver that can:

Decompose complex problems into sub-problems
Decide whether to solve each sub-problem classically or quantumly

By leveraging Meta-Solving, you can:

Identify the most suitable solver (quantum or classical) for each sub-problem
Use quantum computers for sub-problems that are well-suited for them
Leverage classical computers' strengths for sub-problems that are more efficiently solvable classically
Meta-Solving enables a flexible and adaptive approach to hybrid problem-solving, allowing you to:
Optimize resource allocation: By choosing the most suitable solver for each sub-problem, Meta-Solving can optimize resource utilization.
Improve performance: By leveraging quantum computers' strengths, Meta-Solving can speed up computation times for well-suited problems.

Meta-Solving can be an effective strategy for integrating complex quantum features into classical architectures.
