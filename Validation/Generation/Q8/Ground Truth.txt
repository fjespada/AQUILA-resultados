Core Concept A modular framework based on hybrid module designed to encapsulate iterative quantum-classical feedback loops. It ensures seamless interaction between Classical Computing (CC) and Quantum Computing (QC) resources, deployable as either a standalone service or an integrated library.

Key Components * Quantum Component: Handles circuit generation, transpilation, and execution on QPUs or simulators.
    Classical Component: Manages data post-processing, parameter optimization, and state preparation.
    Control Flow Logic: Orchestrates the synchronization and convergence of the iterative loop.

Architectural Pattern: Event-Driven Design * Event Queue: Decouples requests and manages the asynchronous flow between services.
    Control Flow Manager: Dispatches tasks to specialized microservices and maintains the algorithm’s state.

Quality Attributes & NISQ Considerations * Scalability: Independent scaling of classical optimizers and quantum workers.
    Maintainability: Low coupling via standardized messaging interfaces.
    NISQ Readiness: Integrates Error Mitigation (e.g., ZNE, PEC) and hardware-aware optimization to handle noise and decoherence limits.
