In a hybrid quantum-classical system, the classical computing layer plays a crucial role in supporting the quantum computing layer. The primary roles and responsibilities of the classical computing layer include:

Data Management: The classical computing layer is responsible for managing the data that is used to interact with the quantum computer. This includes storing, retrieving, and processing large amounts of classical data.

Control and Monitoring: The classical computing layer acts as a control system, controlling the flow of data between the quantum computer and the outside world. It monitors the quantum computer's performance and makes adjustments as needed.

Error Correction: The classical computing layer is responsible for detecting and correcting errors that may occur during quantum computation. This includes implementing error correction protocols and techniques to mitigate the effects of decoherence and other noise sources.

Classical Post-Processing: After a quantum algorithm has completed, the classical computing layer performs post-processing tasks such as data analysis, pattern recognition, and machine learning algorithms on the results obtained from the quantum computer. 

Communication: The classical computing layer facilitates communication between different components within the hybrid system, including the quantum computer, classical computers, and external users or devices.

In summary, the classical computing layer plays a critical role in supporting the quantum computing layer by managing data, controlling and monitoring the system, correcting errors, and facilitating communication between different components of the hybrid system.
