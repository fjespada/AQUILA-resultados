**Classical Responsibilities**

1. **Compilation Stage**: A classical compiler, Qompiler, takes the unitary matrix U as input and decomposes it into gate-level circuits expressed using an intermediate language (IL). This stage is responsible for:
	* Decomposing quantum circuits into basis gates.
	* Optimizing circuit depth by combining or eliminating gates.
2. **Interpretation Stage**: The classical interpreter, Interpreters, maps IL circuits into native gates for the targeted platforms such as Qiskit, Cirq, or others.

**Quantum Responsibilities**

1. **Quantum Circuit Generation**: A quantum circuit generator takes the optimized IL circuits and generates the corresponding quantum circuits.
2. **Quantum Execution**: The generated quantum circuits are executed on the target quantum device.

**Architecture Smells Elimination**

To eliminate architecture smells, {A'H} incorporates the following design principles:

1. **Separation of Concerns**: Classical compilation and interpretation stages are decoupled from platform-specific dependencies, ensuring cross-platform portability.
2. **Modular Development**: The architecture is designed to facilitate modular development by separating classical and quantum responsibilities.
3. **Optimization**: The optimization stage focuses on decomposing quantum circuits into basis gates and optimizing circuit depth.
