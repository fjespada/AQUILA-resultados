{A'H} is a hybrid architecture designed to eliminate "Circuit Overgrowth" by ensuring quantum tasks remain within the hardware's physical coherence limits (T1​/T2​).

Classical Responsibilities
    Qompiler (Compilation): Decomposes unitaries into Intermediate Language (IL) using Circuit Cutting to partition large circuits into shallower, executable sub-circuits.
    Interpreters (Mapping): Maps IL to native gates using topology-aware routing to minimize noise-inducing SWAP operations.

Quantum Responsibilities
    Execution: Runs optimized circuits on the QPU and monitors fidelity.

Architecture Smells Elimination
    Hardware Blindness: Mitigated by integrating QPU calibration data (error rates) into the transpilation flow.
    Coherence Overrun: Solved by enforcing strict depth limits based on the hardware’s decoherence window.

Refactoring Process

    Identify: Detect circuits exceeding the T1​/T2​ time budget.
    Partition: Apply Circuit Cutting to break down monolithic blocks.
    Optimize: Use gate-level fusion to reduce total execution time.
    Validate: Ensure the final circuit depth is compatible with the target device’s fidelity.
