Firstly, considering the severe noise and qubit limitations of NISQ hardware, the focus is on partitioning classical-quantum workloads effectively. To achieve this, hybrid loops are employed to efficiently delegate tasks between classical CPU and quantum processor (QPU) execution. This approach minimizes the impact of noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) constraints while leveraging the strengths of both classical and quantum computing.

To mitigate errors and enhance reliability, Quantum Error Mitigation (QEM) techniques, such as Zero Noise Extrapolation (ZNE) or Pulse-Level Control (PEC), are utilized. These methods can significantly reduce the noise impact on quantum computations without requiring computationally expensive Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computing (FTQC) or Quantum Error Correction (QEC) codes.

In terms of viability, ensuring that algorithmic strategies are physically executable is paramount. This is achieved by employing hardware-efficient ansatzes and circuit-cutting techniques to optimize quantum circuit designs for specific NISQ constraints.

At the software architecture level, the design focuses on modularizing quantum components to increase reusability across different quantum algorithm implementations. This modular approach enables specialized teams to collaborate effectively with quantum computing experts, while allowing other teams to remain focused on classical software engineering.

On the quantum circuit design level, precision, scalability, and accuracy are prioritized by integrating considerations of both logical quantum algorithm designs and the physical constraints and capabilities of NISQ hardware. This holistic approach ensures the efficient and reliable execution of quantum computations.

In conclusion, the proposed architecture for executing a quantum module on current NISQ hardware prioritizes reliability, viability, and completeness through the strategic combination of hybrid loops, Quantum Error Mitigation techniques, hardware-efficient ansatzes, circuit cutting, modularized quantum components, and a holistic quantum circuit design.
